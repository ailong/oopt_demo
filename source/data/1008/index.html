Geographical location

The forest and forest-steppe belts of low mountains (below 930 m) of the Southern Kraka Mountain Range and Uraltau Ridge in the central part of the Southern Urals.

Major landscapes and ecosystems

Pine, pine-larch (includingsteppe areas), and birch montane forests; pine and larch sparse woodlands; forest and steppe meadows of the forest belt with a history of anthropogenic influence; feathergrass, meadow, and shrub montane steppe.

Species diversity

52 species of mammals; over 190 species of birds; 9 species of amphibians and reptiles; 17 species of fish; about 600 species of butterflies; over 800 species of vascular plants, including endemics of the Ural Mountains and nemoral forests (Lathyrus litvinovii, Knautia tatarica) and relic forms of varying periods (Pliocene - Asarum europaeum, Festuca gigantean, and others, Periglacial - Primula cortusoides, Geranium psilostemon, and others, Holocene - Aster alpinus and others).

Rareplants and animals

Birds: Eastern imperial eagle, golden eagle, osprey, peregrine falcon, Eurasian eagle owl; Fish: Miller's thumb, freshwater sculpin, common and European bullheads, brown trout; Insects: Xylocopa valga, Saga pedo; Butterflies: apollo, clouded apollo, Old World swallowtail; Vascular plants: Minuartia helmii, red helleborine, large-flowered cypripedium, lady's-slipper orchid, prairie junegrass, etc.

Main ecosystem features

The biota has features characteristic of the transitional zone, with European and Siberian components; a significant level of regional endemism and richness of flora and fauna with relic forms of different eras.

Natural attractions

Relic plant communities of montane rocky steppe with concentrations of Ural endemics (Dianthus acicularis, Minuartia helmii, Scutellaria angustifolia, and others).
