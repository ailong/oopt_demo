Geographical location

The southern part of the Central Russian Uplands; southern forest-steppe subzone.

Major landscapes and ecosystems

Upland oak forests; southern upland meadow steppe;steppe grasslands and steppe overgrown with bushes; petrophytic communities from the Cretaceous; floodplain ecosystems.

Species diversity

Over 60 species of mammals; nearly 160 species of birds; about 1,000 species of vascular plants, with relic endemics of the Central Russian Uplands (including Kozo-Polyansky rock jasmine, Bupleurum multinerve, sea grape, and others); over 12,000 identified species of invertebrates.

Rareplants and animals

Insects: stag beetle, hermit beetle: Butterflies: swallowtail, lesser purple emperor, scarce swallowtail, clouded apollo, blue underwing; Plants: Kozo-Polyanskiy rock jasmine, Cypripedium calceolus, fine-leaved peony, Fritillaria ruthenica, Stipa dasyphylla, S. pennata, S. pulcherrima, etc.

Main ecosystem features

Typical ecosystems for the southern European forest-steppe, with all its major natural communities represented; intact areas of upland oak forests and meadow-steppe, as well as a mixture of coniferous-broadleaf, forest-steppe, and steppe communities; a diversity of relic species, including regional endemics mainly associated with Cretaceous formations.

Natural attractions

Ancient oak forest stands, dating back 250-300 years; the world's largest pristine uplands, with intact feathergrass and forb meadow-steppe on black earth soils (Yamskaya Steppe site); chalk hills with petrophytic communities, including relic and endemic plant species; distinctive upland meadow and steppe communities in open areas of the Greater and Sukachevskaya forests of the Vorskla River Site.
